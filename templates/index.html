About the Project

Diseases in crops pose a significant threat to food security. Detection of diseases at early stages of crop cycle when it first appears is very crucial and can save a lot of efforts, time and money of the farmers. The increasing number of smartphones and recent advances in computer vision made possible by deep learning has paved the way for smartphone-assisted disease diagnosis. Using a public dataset of 57,027 images of diseased and healthy plant leaves collected under controlled conditions, a deep convolutional neural network has been trained to identify 15 crop species and 41 diseases (or absence thereof). The trained model achieves an accuracy of 98.97%, demonstrating the feasibility of this approach.

Dataset Description

For this project, 3 different datasets namely “Plant Village dataset”, "PlantDoc dataset" and "UCI ML Ric Leaf Disease dataset" are used. The dataset contains 57,027 images. The images span 15 crop species: Apple, Blueberry, Cherry, Grape, Orange, Peach, Bell Pepper, Potato, Raspberry, Soybean, Squash, Strawberry, Tomato, and Rice. The dataset contains 41 class labels. Each class label is a crop-disease pair, and an attempt is made to predict the crop-disease pair given just the image of the plant leaf. We resize the images to 256 x 256 pixels, and we perform both the model optimization and predictions on these downscaled images. The 41 classes of crop-disease pair are listed below:
